## P4 — The Roommate Wife

**Core:** She's not in crisis — she's in a slow fade. Loves her partner, but the passion quietly leaked out years ago. They don't fight. They just coexist.

**Demographics:** 32–45 | Female | US/UK/AU suburbs | Married 5–15 years, often with kids | Middle to upper-middle class

**Mass Desire:** She wants to want her husband again — to feel chosen, alive, and like a wife instead of a roommate.

**Awareness Stage:** Problem Aware — not actively solution-seeking. She doesn't think she has a "condition." She thinks this is just what marriage becomes.

**Emotions:** Guilty. Like a fraud. Quietly sad. Disconnected — not dramatically, just hollow. Watches friends joke about their sex lives and smiles along while feeling nothing.

**Experiences:**
- Says "not tonight" so often it's become their normal
- Sits next to him on the couch but feels miles away
- Caught herself feeling jealous watching an affectionate couple
- He made a comment — not cruel, but it stung
- Posts anniversary photos and means the love, but knows something's missing

**Objections:**
- Doesn't think she has a problem serious enough to solve
- "This is just what happens after kids, after years, after 35"
- A supplement "for desire" feels too extreme for her situation

**Known Solutions:** Date nights, getaways, maca/ashwagandha, women's multivitamins — nothing targeted at desire

**Deepest Fear:** That this is just forever now. That she'll spend the next 20 years loving him but never wanting him — and he'll eventually feel it too.

**Behavior:**
- Scrolls past "save your marriage" content — that's not her, she's fine
- Googles "is it normal to feel like roommates" at midnight
- Performs normalcy well. No one would know.
- Avoids initiating — the gap is too wide to bridge casually

**Failed Solutions:** Date nights, getaways, willpower, generic supplements, wine, addressing the relationship (not the body)

**Messaging Tone:** Non-serious, fun, hype. Make the product feel like something exciting to try — not a fix for a serious problem. The emotion should be exciting, not clinical.

## P5 — The Menopause-Rejected Woman

**Core:** A furious, solution-seeking woman dismissed by her doctor with "it's just menopause" — and refuses to accept it. She's not fading quietly; she's actively hunting.

**Demographics:** 47–60 | Female | Often empty-nester or nearly there | Established career or comfortable retirement | Higher disposable income | US/UK/AU

**Mass Desire:** To feel vital, desired, and like herself again. Proof that desire doesn't end at 50.

**Awareness Stage:** Solution Aware — actively seeking. She knows it's hormonal. She's Googled. She's tried things. Frustrated, not defeated.

**Emotions:** Furious. Dismissed. Invisible — to doctors, sometimes to her partner, sometimes to herself. Misses who she was. Refuses to accept this is permanent. The anger is louder than the sadness.

**Experiences:**
- Went to doctor about low desire and was told "that's just menopause" — sent home
- Tried HRT: helped hot flashes, didn't touch desire
- Tried prescription libido meds: side effects, no real results
- Bought supplements that promised everything, delivered nothing
- Feels invisible in a medical system that treats women over 50 as a lost cause
- Worries her relationship is quietly suffering while she fights this alone

**Objections:**
- Already spent money on things that didn't work. Guard is up.
- Needs to believe this is actually different — not just another vague supplement
- Doesn't want to be treated as a "menopausal woman" — just a woman

**Known Solutions:** Addyi/prescriptions, HRT, health store menopause blends, expensive wellness products, therapy

**Deepest Fear:** That this is actually permanent. That the version of herself she's fighting to get back is just gone. That everyone who dismissed her was right.

**Behavior:**
- Actively researches — not waiting for solutions to find her
- Pushes back on doctors, seeks second opinions
- Refuses to be defined by her age or diagnosis
- Doesn't post publicly but reads everything privately
- Shows up fully in every other area of life — this is the one thing she can't solve

**Failed Solutions:** HRT, prescription medication, health store blends, expensive wellness products, therapy — everything treated symptoms or psychology, nothing addressed the biological mechanism

**Messaging Tone:** Validation-first. Credibility is everything. Speak to the fury before the fix. She's not looking for hope — she's looking for proof.

## P6 — The Time-Aware Woman

**Core:** A woman who has quietly accepted the disconnect for too long — and just hit her breaking point. Done waiting for it to fix itself. Ready to take one concrete action, right now.

**Demographics:** 35–52 | Female | US/UK/AU | Psychological state, not strict demographic — can overlap with other personas

**Mass Desire:** She wants to finally do something — and have it actually work. A concrete action that breaks the pattern she's been stuck in.

**Awareness Stage:** Highly Problem Aware → Crossing into Solution Seeking. She's not browsing — she's deciding.

**Emotions:** Frustrated with herself for waiting so long. Guilty about time lost. But mostly — determined. The sadness has curdled into resolve.

**Experiences:**
- Told herself it would get better — for over a year, maybe several
- Watched time pass while quietly hoping something would shift on its own
- Hit a moment of clarity: if it was going to fix itself, it would have
- Looks back and can't believe how long she let this go on
- Fears that without action, she'll be having this same conversation with herself next year

**Objections:**
- Paralysis from being passive so long — deciding feels hard
- Second-guesses whether this is "the right thing" — not doubting the product, just out of practice at acting

**Known Solutions:** Probably date nights, maybe therapy. Or nothing — she'd accepted it as reality. Time was her "solution," and time failed.

**Deepest Fear:** That she'll still be here next year. Same problem, same paralysis, another year gone.

**Behavior:**
- Has been quietly passive — not in denial, just stuck
- Hits moments of frustration or clarity, then lets them pass — until now
- When she finally acts, the act itself feels like relief before results arrive
- Buys as a commitment to herself, not just a product trial

**Failed Solutions:** Date nights, therapy, time itself — she waited, hoped, deferred — and nothing external changed

**Messaging Tone:** Urgency as empowerment. The act of buying is the decision to change. Speak to the moment of resolve.

## P7 — The Post-Hysterectomy Woman

**Core:** A woman who had a hysterectomy and feels like her body stole her desire overnight. Not slowly fading — she feels broken. Desperately solution-aware but hasn't found anything that actually works.

**Demographics:** 35–54 | Female

**Mass Desire:** To get back her desire for sex.

**Awareness Stage:** Solution Aware — knows there are solutions but hasn't found the right one. Needs convincing that Bloomin is the perfect fit.

**Emotions:**
- "I hate it, not feeling anything. I used to initiate most of the time and now I just feel nothing."
- "My body is betraying me and I feel like such a disappointment."
- Guilty. Sad. Angry. Broken.

**Experiences:**
- Very low libido
- Vaginal dryness
- Difficulty orgasming
- Hard conversations with partner reassuring him he is not the problem

**Objections:**
- "It takes 6–8 months for hormones to balance post-op. Hopefully it'll spring back."
- Believing HRT will solve it
- Taking estrogen/testosterone supplements
- Trying marijuana

**Known Solutions:** HRT, estrogen, testosterone

**Deepest Fear:**
- Never getting back desire for her partner
- Partner leaving because they're never intimate
- Vaginal cuff complications making sex even harder

**Behavior:**
- Uses lube before sex
- Drinks wine before partner arrives to relax
- Barely thinks about sex — desire is gone
- Doesn't initiate

**Failed Solutions:** Waiting out hormonal rebalancing, HRT, estrogen/testosterone — root cause unaddressed biologically

**Messaging Tone:** Empathy for feeling broken. Mechanism clarity — explain why desire shutdown happened and why Bloomin targets that specific pathway.

## P8 — The Nexplanon Woman

**Core:** A young woman whose birth control implant killed her libido overnight. Knows exactly what caused it. Feels guilty watching it damage her relationship. Stuck between removing the implant and finding something that restores desire alongside it.

**Demographics:** 15–49 | Female | In a relationship (1–5 years, engaged, or newly married) | Pre-kids or actively preventing pregnancy | Middle class, often dual-income

**Mass Desire:** To get back her desire for sex.

**Awareness Stage:** Solution Aware — knows exactly what caused it. Looking for something that works alongside her birth control. Needs convincing Bloomin can restore what the implant suppressed — without interfering with contraception.

**Emotions:**
- "I have zero libido and no desire to be intimate with my husband. That's nerve-wracking."
- "It caused problems in my relationship — my boyfriend thinks I'm not attracted to him, but it's not that."
- Moody. Irritable. Ashamed toward partner.

**Experiences:**
- Complete loss of libido since getting the implant
- Irregular bleeding / constant spotting
- Weight gain
- Increased anxiety
- Insomnia
- Emotionally flat or numb
- Hard conversations reassuring partner it's not about him
- Dismissed by doctors: "that's a normal side effect"

**Objections:**
- "Removing the Nexplanon will fix everything"
- "My body just needs to adjust"
- "Switching birth control will solve it"
- "Nothing can help while the implant is still in"

**Known Solutions:** Remove implant, switch BC, wait it out, talk to doctor (who usually dismisses), supplements (skeptical they'd work alongside implant)

**Deepest Fear:**
- Partner thinks she doesn't love him — lack of sex as proof
- He doesn't feel attractive to her
- She'll lose the relationship over this

**Behavior:**
- Barely thinks about sex — desire completely gone
- Never initiates
- Constantly reassures partner it's not about him
- Considering implant removal but dreading the hassle
- Scrolls Reddit at night looking for answers

**Failed Solutions:** Waiting for adjustment, trying to push through, no targeted solution that works alongside the implant

**Messaging Tone:** Relief that there's an answer that doesn't require removing her contraception. No interference messaging is key.

## P9 — The Perimenopause Woman

**Core:** A high-functioning woman in her late 30s–mid 40s who feels like she's slowly falling apart — exhausted, irritable, foggy, disconnected — but blames stress or aging instead of perimenopause. Needs to first recognize herself in the problem.

**Demographics:** 38–48 | Female | Long-term relationship or marriage, often with school-age kids | Middle to upper-middle class, dual-income | US/UK/AU

**Mass Desire:** She wants to feel like herself again.

**Awareness Stage:** Unaware → Problem Aware. In unaware stage: blames stress, burnout, anxiety, poor sleep, aging. In problem-aware stage: may suspect hormones but feels confused, dismissed, overwhelmed. Not solution-aware. Not searching for menopause supplements or libido support.

**Emotions:** Anxious. Irritable. Overstimulated. Ashamed around intimacy. Guilty toward partner. Quietly scared something is seriously wrong. "I don't feel like myself and I can't explain why."

**Experiences:**
- Night wakings 3–4am with anxiety or panic
- Chronic poor sleep
- Brain fog and word-finding issues
- Sudden irritability, rage, emotional swings
- Loss of motivation or pleasure
- Decreased libido and lack of spontaneous desire
- Feeling disconnected from her body
- Dismissed by doctors ("it's stress," "it's normal," "welcome to aging")
- Trying to function normally while feeling internally dysregulated

**Objections:**
- "This is just stress — I should be able to fix this myself."
- "I don't have a hormone problem."
- "Supplements don't really work."
- "I just need better sleep / lifestyle changes."
- "If it was hormones, my doctor would have said something."

**Known Solutions:** Not well-informed. Has tried lifestyle fixes, not targeted hormonal support.

**Deepest Fear:** That this is permanent. That she's losing herself. That her brain is failing. That her relationship will suffer. That she'll never feel desire again. That no one takes her seriously because she's "too young."

**Behavior:**
- Pushes through exhaustion
- Barely thinks about sex
- Rarely initiates intimacy
- Reassures partner it's not about him
- Snaps, then feels guilty
- Googles symptoms at night
- Tries to "fix herself" quietly
- Avoids labels like menopause or libido problems

**Failed Solutions:** Sleep hygiene, cutting caffeine/sugar/alcohol, exercise, magnesium/melatonin, antidepressants, therapy, mindfulness — root cause: doesn't yet understand that her symptoms are connected, or that perimenopause can start in her late 30s

**Messaging Tone:** Pattern recognition. Connect the dots for her — show her that her scattered symptoms are one story, not many. Don't lead with menopause or libido. Lead with "does this sound like you?"

## P10 — The Shut-Out Husband

**Core:** A man who feels invisible and rejected in his own marriage. Not because he wants more sex — because he wants his wife to want him again. Tried everything. His deepest fear is that this is just his life now.

**Demographics:** 25–55 | Male | Married or long-term relationship (5–30+ years) | Often has kids | Middle to upper-middle class | Suburbs

**Mass Desire:** I want my wife to want me again.

**Awareness Stage:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware

**Emotions:** Insecure. Confused. Feels dirty for asking. Hurt. Taken advantage of. Depressed. Rejected and disconnected. Frustrated. Unwanted. "I've told her so many times how it destroys my confidence."

**Experiences:**
- Wife is worried his high sex drive means he'll cheat
- Pushed out of the bed
- Excuses: "I'm too tired, I'm gassy, I'm too full"
- "She told me she's been having sex with me because she feels it's her duty."
- Usually encounters eye-rolling, deep sighs, or outright rejection

**Objections:**
- Getting wife to therapy or a doctor
- Getting wife on medication
- Seeing a hormone doctor

**Known Solutions:** Date nights, hotel getaways, sex toys, more housework, romantic texts, non-sexual touch

**Deepest Fear:**
- Wife doesn't love him anymore — lack of sex as proof
- She doesn't find him attractive
- She's having an affair
- She'll eventually leave
- A comfortable, celibate half-life
- He'll cheat — not because he wants to, but because the need becomes unbearable
- Kids growing up in a broken home
- He wasted his sexual prime never being truly wanted

**Behavior:**
- Stopped initiating — rejection is too painful
- Masturbates regularly, feels sad about it
- Can't get aroused because her disinterest is a turn-off
- Throws himself into work, hobbies, kids to avoid thinking about it

**Failed Solutions:** Dates, hotel weekends, reducing her stress, hard conversations, suggesting therapy (refused or ineffective), suggesting doctor visits (dismissed), non-sexual touch, flirty texts, new bedroom ideas, wine — nothing addressed the physical root cause

**Messaging Tone:** Practical. Speak to his helplessness — he's tried everything. Position Bloomin as the missing piece he couldn't find: something that addresses her biology, not their relationship. He's not the problem. The mechanism is.

## P11 — The New Mom

**Core:** A new mom who has lost more than her libido — she's lost herself. Exhausted, touched out, quietly falling behind on intimacy while telling herself it'll come back on its own.

**Demographics:** 26–40 | Female | 3–24 months postpartum, often breastfeeding or recently stopped | Married or long-term relationship | Middle to upper-middle income | Health- and wellness-oriented, prefers natural solutions

**Mass Desire:** She wants herself back. Not just libido — her old identity.

**Awareness Stage:** Problem Aware — not mechanism-aware.

**Emotions:** Guilty (feels like she's letting partner down). Confused. Insecure — doesn't feel attractive. Frustrated and stuck. Scared (stories of it taking 4 years back frighten her). Disconnected from closeness and emotional intimacy.

**Experiences:**
- Hormonal shifts postpartum
- Chronic fatigue and sleep deprivation
- Complete loss of libido
- Waiting for it to come back on its own
- Trying to fix the relationship instead of her body
- Pushing herself into sex without desire
- Trying supplements without results

**Objections:**
- "It will come back after I'm done nursing."
- "It's just hormonal / it's just exhaustion."
- "Libido supplements don't work."

**Known Solutions:** Libido supplements, hormones, waiting it out

**Deepest Fear:** That this is permanent. Losing intimacy. Losing her identity. Losing her femininity.

**Behavior:**
- Avoids initiating — rarely feels desire, worries it will feel forced
- Sometimes allows intimacy out of guilt
- Pulls away from physical touch at end of day — touched out and overstimulated
- Googles "when does libido come back postpartum" late at night
- Reads Reddit and mom forums for reassurance
- Keeps telling herself it will come back instead of solving it
- Blames hormones or breastfeeding rather than seeing it as changeable
- Uncomfortable with her body, avoids exposure
- Prioritizes baby, household, responsibilities before herself
- Sometimes pretends to be more tired to avoid intimacy
- Considers solutions but delays — hopes time will fix it

**Failed Solutions:** Waiting for natural return, pushing herself into intimacy without desire, generic supplements, fixing the relationship instead of the body

**Messaging Tone:** Identity restoration. Not "fix your libido" — "get yourself back." Non-guilt. Speak to the loss of self first, then the solution.

## P12 — The PCOS Woman

**Core:** A woman whose PCOS has quietly stolen her desire, arousal, and confidence — and who fears it's permanent because PCOS has no cure. Tried everything, nothing worked. Deepest fear: PCOS took both her fertility and her sexuality.

**Demographics:** Teens–30s | Female | Strong online community (r/PCOS, 150k+ members) | 60% report little to no arousal or sexual desire

**Mass Desire:** She wants to want sex again. And to get rid of vaginal dryness.

**Awareness Stage:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware

**Emotions:** Scared something is fundamentally wrong with her. Worried she might be asexual. Sad. Hopeless. Isolated. Frustrated.

**Experiences:**
- Vaginal dryness
- Hard conversations reassuring partner he's not the problem
- Tried watching porn or reading smut to force arousal
- Irregular periods
- Painful sex, sometimes bleeding during or after
- Difficulty getting physically aroused
- Inability to orgasm
- Put on birth control as a "bandaid" since teens (which killed libido further)
- Pelvic pain from ovarian cysts during sex
- Depression and/or anxiety

**Objections:**
- "Losing weight will help."
- "Estrogen creams will fix it."
- "Maca root is enough."
- "Counselling will address it."
- "Consistent training will regulate my hormones."
- "Nothing can truly help because PCOS has no cure."

**Known Solutions:** Lubrication, maca root, supplements/gummies, THC lube, doctor/sex therapist, inositol, metformin, vibrators

**Deepest Fear:** That she's asexual. That her partner will leave or have an affair. That she can never have normal sex. That PCOS permanently broke her desire and took both her fertility and her ability to enjoy sex.

**Behavior:** Never initiates sex.

**Failed Solutions:** Birth control pills, vitamins, metformin, doctor/sex therapist, antidepressants, Olly libido supplements, nutritionist, maca root, ashwagandha

**Messaging Tone:** Hopeful but evidence-based. Speak to the fear that PCOS makes her a lost cause. Then directly dismantle it with mechanism — her desire was suppressed, not destroyed.
