# Bloomin Gummies — Product & Hypothesis
_Recently launched. Save and reference for all gummy-related strategy and copy._

---

## 1. Positioning

Gummies don't replace honey sticks. They're two different entry points into the same journey.

| Product | Role | Who she is |
|---------|------|------------|
| **Honey Sticks** | The ritual. Intentional, therapeutic, a daily moment. | Already bought in, wants the experience |
| **Gummies** | The easiest way to start. Daily, on-the-go, zero friction. | Cold traffic, first-time buyer, skeptical |

**Same UMS. Same formula. Same promise. Different moment, different woman.**

No sub-brand. No separate name. Just **"Bloomin Gummies."**

---

## 2. Hypothesis

**Primary: gummies lower the barrier for cold traffic → higher CVR, lower CPA.**

Secondary: easier format → better compliance → better retention at 60 days.

---

## 3. What We Measure

Not 10 metrics at once. Just two:
1. **CPA** (cost per acquisition) — does the gummy format convert cold traffic cheaper?
2. **Retention at 60 days** — does the easier format improve compliance and stick rate?

---

## 4. Implications for Copy & Strategy

- Lead with **zero friction** for gummy ads: _"No rituals. No prep. Just two gummies."_
- Same UMS arc (Diagnosis → Disqualification → Breakthrough → Permission) — just shorter ritual language
- Don't position gummies as "less than" honey sticks — different entry point, same destination
- Gummies = acquisition vehicle. Honey sticks = loyalty/ritual vehicle.
- Test gummy creative against honey stick creative on cold traffic — CPA is the deciding metric
